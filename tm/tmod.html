Modelling The Trajectory Of The Spotlight Sweep

The spotlight must start from a predefined position and end in another predefined position for each sweep. These positions are randomly generated before the sweep animation ensues.

The type of trajectory is important. To be more specific, we should form a triangle between the axis of the spotlight, and the start and end points of the trajectory. We then check if we are able to draw a line from the axis of the spotlight to the trajectory line in a way where it is perpendicular to that trajectory line.

If its possible to draw a perpendicular line, then our program splits the trajectory into 2 journeys and initiates them in a sequence. This is because our angular velocity does not a follow a simple deceleration or acceleration. It instead follows a curve. Because the halo of the spotlight gets closer and closer to the emitter of the spotlight right until a certain point (which is the point where the perpendicular line interesects the trajectory line) and then the halo gets further and further away.

We can use that intersection point as a the pivot to decide how to structure our entire sweep animation
